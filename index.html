Who We Are

Uganda's Strategic
Petroleum Partner

The Uganda National Oil Company Limited (UNOC) was incorporated in 2015 under the Petroleum (Exploration, Development and Production) Act, 2013.

UNOC holds and manages the Government of Uganda's commercial interests in the petroleum sector — participating in joint ventures with international oil companies, developing midstream infrastructure, and building national petroleum capacity for long-term economic transformation.

🎯 Mission

Profitably developing Uganda's petroleum resources to generate maximum long-term value for all Ugandans.

🌍 Vision

Becoming a significant contributor to Uganda's socio-economic transformation through petroleum.

⚖️ Integrity

Operating with transparency, accountability and the highest ethical standards in all we do.

🏆 Excellence

Delivering world-class results through innovation, expertise and continuous improvement.

Our Mandate

What UNOC Does
for Uganda

⛏️

Upstream Participation

UNOC holds the Government's equity in Production Sharing Agreements in the Albertine Graben — partnering with TotalEnergies and CNOOC across Tilenga, Kingfisher and other licensed blocks.

🔧

Refining & Midstream

Through URHC, UNOC is developing Uganda's first oil refinery at Kabaale and holds interest in the East African Crude Oil Pipeline (EACOP) — the world's longest electrically heated crude oil pipeline.

⚡

Capacity Building

Developing the next generation of Ugandan petroleum professionals through internships, international secondments, sponsored postgraduate training and structured career programmes.

FAQs

Common
Questions

Everything you need to know about UNOC, Uganda's petroleum sector and how to engage with us.

UNOC is the Government of Uganda's commercial instrument in the petroleum sector. It holds and manages the government's equity interests in upstream joint ventures (Tilenga and Kingfisher), and through its subsidiaries holds interests in the EACOP pipeline and the planned Uganda Oil Refinery.
Both the Tilenga Project (operated by TotalEnergies) and Kingfisher Area Development (operated by CNOOC) are targeting First Oil in the mid-2020s. The EACOP pipeline, which will transport the crude oil to Tanzania's port of Tanga, is under construction in parallel.
Ugandan businesses can access opportunities through the National Content Secretariat (under the Petroleum Authority of Uganda) and through UNOC's own procurement processes. Categories include catering, security, civil construction, transport, equipment supply and professional services. Registration in the National Supplier Database is required.
Yes. UNOC runs an annual Graduate Internship Programme open to Ugandan graduates in petroleum engineering, geosciences, finance, law, IT and related fields. Applications are announced on the UNOC website and major job boards. UNOC also arranges international secondments to joint venture partner companies for senior technical staff.
All UNOC tenders are published on the UNOC website under the Tenders section and in the Uganda Gazette. UNOC follows the Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets (PPDA) Act, ensuring open, competitive and transparent procurement processes for all contracts.
UNOC enforces ESIA (Environmental and Social Impact Assessment) compliance across all projects. We are committed to zero routine flaring, biodiversity protection in the Albertine Rift, and transparent EITI reporting. UNOC works closely with the National Environment Management Authority (NEMA) and international environmental standards bodies.
