Who We Are

Uganda's Strategic
Petroleum Partner

The Uganda National Oil Company Limited (UNOC) was incorporated in 2015 under the Petroleum (Exploration, Development and Production) Act, 2013 and the Petroleum (Refining, Conversion, Transmission and Midstream Storage) Act, 2013.

UNOC holds and manages the government's commercial interests in the petroleum sector, participates in joint ventures with international oil companies, and develops the country's petroleum value chain for long-term economic transformation.

🎯 Mission

To profitably develop Uganda's petroleum resources to generate maximum long-term value for Uganda.

🌍 Vision

To be a significant contributor to Uganda's socio-economic development through petroleum resources.

⚖️ Integrity

Operating with transparency, accountability and the highest ethical standards in all activities.

🏆 Excellence

Delivering world-class results through innovation, expertise and continuous improvement.

Our Mandate

What UNOC Does for Uganda

⛏️

Upstream Participation

UNOC holds the government's equity interest in production sharing agreements in the Albertine Graben, partnering with TotalEnergies, CNOOC, and other operators across Tilenga, Kingfisher and other licensed areas.

🔧

Refining & Midstream

Through its subsidiary Uganda Refinery Holding Company Limited (URHC), UNOC is at the forefront of developing Uganda's first oil refinery at Kabaale, and holds interest in the East African Crude Oil Pipeline (EACOP).

⚡

National Capacity Building

UNOC develops Ugandan petroleum expertise through targeted internship programmes, secondments to international oil companies, and supporting the growth of local content in the oil and gas sector.

Projects & Operations

Where We Work

UNOC has equity participation in three major upstream projects in the Albertine Graben, plus critical midstream infrastructure.

Upstream · Operated by TotalEnergies EP Uganda

Tilenga Project

Located in the Albertine Graben, the Tilenga Project spans blocks 1, 1A, 2 and 3A. It includes development of the Jobi-Rii, Gunya, Ngiri and associated fields, as well as critical infrastructure inside Murchison Falls National Park.

East African Crude Oil Pipeline

The world's longest electrically heated crude oil pipeline runs 1,443 km from Hoima, Uganda to Tanga Port, Tanzania. UNOC through its subsidiary UNOC Pipeline Company (UPC) holds Uganda's equity stake in this transformative infrastructure.

Joint Venture Partners

World-Class Partners
in Uganda's Growth

UNOC collaborates with leading global energy companies to develop Uganda's petroleum resources, bringing world-class technology, capital and expertise.

Tilenga Project Operator

TotalEnergies EP Uganda operates the Tilenga Project — one of the largest upstream investments in sub-Saharan Africa, developing blocks 1, 1A, 2 and 3A in the Albertine Graben.

Kingfisher Area Operator

CNOOC Uganda Limited operates the Kingfisher Area Development (KAD) in Block 2 — covering the Kingfisher and Kakindo fields in one of East Africa's most significant oil developments.

Strategic Commercial Partners

UNOC engages with a range of strategic commercial partners for offtake, trading and downstream activities — ensuring Uganda's oil reaches international markets efficiently and competitively.

Maximising Ugandan Value

UNOC is committed to ensuring that Uganda's petroleum development generates lasting socio-economic benefits for Ugandan citizens, businesses and communities.

Ugandan Business Opportunities

UNOC works with the National Content Secretariat and IOC partners to ensure Ugandan companies access contracts in catering, construction, logistics, transportation, equipment supply and professional services across all project phases.

02

Skills & Workforce Development

Through the UNOC Internship Programme, secondments and the Makerere University partnership, UNOC is building a world-ready generation of Ugandan petroleum engineers, geologists, economists and lawyers.

UNOC's CSR programmes support host communities in Hoima, Kikuube and Buliisa districts — investing in health, clean water, education infrastructure and livelihood programmes alongside petroleum operations.
